Varicella Vaccine (Chickenpox)

Age Group:

The Varicella vaccine is typically administered as follows:

  • Children: First dose at 12-15 months, second dose at 4-6 years.
  • Older Children & Adults (if not previously vaccinated): Two doses, at least 4-8 weeks apart.
  • People 13 years or older: 2 doses (0.5 ml each) of the single-antigen varicella vaccine subcutaneously, 4-8 weeks apart.
  • Note: MMRV vaccine is not approved for people in this age group.

Benefits:

  • Prevents infection with chickenpox (varicella): A severe skin infection that can lead to complications such as pneumonia and brain inflammation.
  • Prevents later-life complications: Reduces the risk of developing shingles later in life.
  • Reduces the risk of hospitalization and severe disease: Helps prevent serious complications from varicella infection.
  • Prevents the spread of the virus within the community: Helps control the spread of varicella infections.

Number of Doses:

The Varicella vaccine is typically given as follows:

  • Children: 2 doses, one at 12–15 months and another at 4–6 years of age.
  • Older kids, teens, and adults without immunity: 2 doses, 4–8 weeks apart.
  • Special Cases: Healthcare professionals, teachers, and other high-risk employees need two doses if they're not immune.
  • Pregnant women: Should wait until after childbirth to get the vaccine, if necessary.

Side Effects:

The Varicella vaccine is generally safe, but like all vaccines, it may cause some side effects:

  • Mild: Redness, small bumps, pain at the injection site, and mild fever.
  • Moderate: Temporary joint pain and swollen lymph nodes.
  • Serious but unlikely: Severe allergic reactions, pneumonia, or low platelets.
