Typhoid Conjugate Vaccine (TCV) + Menactra (Meningococcal Conjugate Vaccine) Overview

Age Group:

TCV: Single dose (0.5 mL IM) from 6 months of age; booster after 3 years if needed.

  • 9–23 months: Two doses (0.5 mL IM) 3 months apart.
  • 2–55 years: Single dose (0.5 mL IM). Booster every 5 years for those at risk.



Benefits:

  • Enhanced coverage: Protects against typhoid and meningococcal diseases.
  • Improved compliance: Fewer visits improve vaccination rates.
  • Safe and effective: Studies show co-administration does not reduce immune response.
  • Program efficiency: Streamlines vaccination programs, ideal for resource-limited settings.

Doses:

1.Typhoid Conjugate Vaccine (TCV):

    • Recommended age: Starting from 6 months of age.
    • Dosage: A single dose of 0.5 mL is given intramuscularly.
    • Booster dose: Currently, a booster is not routinely recommended, but if necessary, a booster can be given after 3 years.

2. Menactra (Meningococcal Conjugate Vaccine):

  • Recommended age:
    • 9 months to 23 months: Two doses of 0.5 mL each, administered 3 months apart.
    • 2 years to 55 years: A single dose of 0.5 mL is given intramuscularly.

Side Effects:

1. Typhoid Conjugate Vaccine (TCV)

  • Common: Pain, redness, swelling, mild fever, fatigue, headache.
  • Rare: Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis).

2. Menactra

  • Common: Soreness, mild fever, headache, fatigue, muscle pain.
  • Rare: Severe allergic reaction, fainting, Guillain-Barré Syndrome (very rare).
