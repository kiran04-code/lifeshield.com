Cervical Cancer Vaccine (HPV Vaccine) Overview

Age Group:

1. 11–12 years: Ideal age for vaccina on (before HPV exposure).

2. 9–14 years: 2-dose schedule (6–12 months apart).

3. 15–26 years: 3-dose schedule (at 0, 1–2, and 6 months).

4. 27–45 years: 3-dose schedule; vaccina on based on individual risk.




Benefits:

1. Prevents Cervical Cancer: Protects against high-risk HPV types (16, 18, etc.) causing 90% of cervical cancers.

2. Protects Against Other Cancers: Vaginal, vulvar, anal, throat, and penile cancers.

3. Prevents Genital Warts: Covers HPV-6 and HPV-11 (90% of genital warts cases).

4. Herd Immunity: Reduces HPV spread in the population.

5. Long-lasting Immunity: Protection lasts at least 10 years, possibly lifelong.

6. Fewer Medical Interventions: Reduces need for Pap tests, colposcopies, and precancer treatment.

7. Safe and Effective: Well-tested, mild side effects common.

Doses:

The doses of cervical cancer vaccines depend on the age at which vaccina on is ini ated. The vaccines available include Gardasil, Gardasil 9, and Cervarix.

1. Age 9 to 14 Years (Two-Dose Schedule):

  • Number of doses: 2 doses

2. Age 15 to 26 Years (Three-Dose Schedule):

  • Number of doses: 3 doses
  • 1st dose: At the chosen date.
  • 2nd dose: 1 to 2 months after the first dose.
  • 3rd dose: 6 months after the first dose.

Side Effects:

• Common:

  • Injection site pain, redness, swelling.
  • Fatigue, mild fever, headache, nausea, muscle/joint pain.

• Less Common:

  • Mild allergic reactions (rash, itching).

• Rare:

  • No evidence of serious long-term health problems.
