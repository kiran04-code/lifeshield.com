Influenza

Age Group:

Influenza vaccines are recommended for individuals starting from 6 months of age and older. Annual vaccination is advised for everyone, particularly for those at higher risk of complications, such as young children, pregnant women, the elderly, and individuals with chronic health conditions.




Benefits:

The influenza vaccine protects against seasonal flu viruses, which can cause mild to severe illness and, in some cases, lead to hospitalization or death. The flu vaccine is updated annually to target the most common and anticipated strains of the influenza virus for the upcoming flu season. Key benefits include:

  • Prevention of flu illness: Reduces the risk of getting sick with the flu and its associated symptoms, such as fever, cough, sore throat, fatigue, and body aches.
  • Reduced severity: If vaccinated individuals contract the flu, their illness is typically milder and shorter in duration.
  • Protection for vulnerable groups: Helps protect high-risk individuals, such as infants, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems.
  • Reduced spread: By lowering the chances of infection, the vaccine helps minimize the spread of influenza in the community, contributing to herd immunity.

Doses:

Influenza vaccine 1- 6 months

Influenza vaccine 1- 6 months

The dosing schedule depends on the age and vaccination history of the individual:

  • 6 months to 8 years old (first-time vaccination): Children in this age group who are receiving the flu vaccine for the first time need two doses at least 4 weeks apart.
  • 6 months and older (annual vaccination): A single dose of the influenza vaccine is recommended annually for everyone in this age group.
  • Inactivated Influenza Vaccine (IIV): Injected form of the vaccine, suitable for individuals aged 6 months and older.

Side Effects:

Most people experience mild side effects, if any, after receiving the influenza vaccine. These side effects typically resolve within a few days and may include:

  • Pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site.
  • Mild fever, headache, or muscle aches.
  • Runny nose or congestion (for nasal spray vaccines).
  • Severe allergic reactions are extremely rare, but individuals with a history of severe allergies to any vaccine component should consult their healthcare provider before receiving the flu vaccine.
