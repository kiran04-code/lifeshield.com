Tdap

Age Group:

1. Tdap:

  • 11–12 years: Single dose recommended.
  • 13–18 years: Catch-up dose if not previously vaccinated.
  • 19+ years: Single dose for those never vaccinated, especially pregnant women (27–36 weeks) & infant caregivers.
  • Booster: Td every 10 years after Tdap.

2. Td:

  • 7+ years: Booster dose every 10 years or after 5 years for wound management.



Benefits:

1. Prevents Tetanus: Protects against painful muscle spasms and respiratory failure.

2. Prevents Diphtheria: Reduces risk of severe respiratory disease and organ damage.

3. Prevents Pertussis: Protects against whooping cough, especially in infants.

4. Herd Immunity: Reduces disease spread, protecting vulnerable groups.

5. Pregnancy Protection: Tdap during pregnancy prevents whooping cough in newborns.

6. Long-term Immunity: Provides protection with boosters every 10 years.

Doses:

1. Tdap:

  • First dose: At 11–12 years or catch-up at any age.
  • Booster: Td every 10 years.

2. Td:

  • Given as a booster after initial Tdap.
  • Wound management: Booster if >5 years since last dose.

Side Effects:

• Common:

  • Injection site pain, redness, swelling.
  • Mild fever, fatigue, headache, muscle/joint pain.

• Less Common:

  • Mild allergic reactions (rash, itching), fainting (especially in teens).

• Management:

  • Apply cold compress, use pain relievers for mild reactions.
  • Seek medical help for severe reactions.

Safe and effective, Tdap/Td vaccines offer essential protection against serious diseases.
